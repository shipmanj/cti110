Some states may have their own laws in regards to gambling in addition to any federal regulation that exists.
Lootboxes function the same way as a slot machine or the state lottery but with a few very distinct differences.
One of the most notable distinctions is that minors are now allowed to even enter a casino or participate in state lotteries.
I am actually unaware of any places in the USA where minors are legally allowed to gamble.
These kinds of rules however do not apply to lootboxes or other forms of microtransactions and that's what makes them popular with video game companies.

What is a lootbox and where is my dragon shield?

A lootbox is like the slot machine in a casino but it's more accessible than a slot machine is.
They usually give out some kind of prize such as a character skin or an item of some sort.
The other thing of note is that they can be found in many video games that one could buy online or in a store.
There's also no limit on how much someone can spend on these video games either, all it takes is a linked credit card.
Worst still is that many gaves featuring this actively try to attract kids with flashy colors and cosmetics.
Factor in a possible lack of supervision by some parents and this can result in thousands of dollars being spent in as little as 5 minutes.
Smartphones stores themselves are bevy of mobile games designed purely around the purpose of getting people to buy lootboxes and other forms of MTX.
An article from the gaming news outlet Game Rant corroborates with a story of a 6 year old child spending over $16,000 on a mobile game. To a large amount of people, that is a lot of money.
Here's another article from a more general news outlet Forbes that explains in detail how kids are intentionally targeted in order to siphon money from what is most likely unaware parents.
It's gotten so egregious that other countries have now started to look into regulating if not outright banning lootboxes entirely. The previously linked Forbes article also mentions this.
This isn't the only thing these companies use to siphon money however and it is debatable if it's one their worst tactics.

Lootboxes are common but they aren't the only types of predatory MTX that exists.
Anything in a video game can be monetized and companies will absolutely do it if they can get away with it.
What if you keep failing to upgrade an amazing sword you acquired? Will you keep trying to upgrade it and fail? What if failing the upgrade destroys it?
Well why not just pay money to make the upgrade chance 100%?
Do you only have some many mission passes for the day before you have to wait for more the next day?
Well why not just pay for more?
Even many games that modes that pit two people against each can sell you power just so you can become stronger than your opponent and never lose.
In a rather humorous set of circumstances, an article from ExtremeTech a player for DiabloImmortal did this very thing. The result being the game could not find an opponent to match against him.
